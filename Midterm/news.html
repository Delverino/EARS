Students Storm Out of School, Excited for Summer
   by Russell Brown - June 5, 2007
What time is it? Summertime! Students at East High started the summer break by breaking out into song with elaborate choreography, trashing the school in the process. "I don't know what I did to deserve this," says Frank Stewart, the school's only on-duty janitor toady. "I was wondering why all of my coworkers chose today for their day off...apparently this is a regular thing the kids do." This was Frank's first year working at East High - and, unfortunately, his last year of working at all. Shortly after the interview, a basketball was thrown at Stewart's head, knocking him unconcious and resulting in a coma he still hasn't woken up from yet. "It was just part of our basketball team number," says Troy Bolton, a self-proclaimed "main character" of the school. "He should have known we'd be throwing the basketballs offstage, we had to get rid of them before the transition into the chorus."
Why Do Our Students Look Middle-Aged? An Expert Explains
   by Kate Oswald - May 23, 2007
You may take a look at our students and wonder: what are these full on adults doing lurking around a high school? And what did they do to the real students?? Before you call the police, we can assure you that there's a perfectly sound explanation. To teach you about this strange phenomenon at East High, we interviewed an expert on aging, Deborah Watson.

"Well, the reason why these students look middle aged is because, well...they are. This school has the worst graduation rate in the country, some of your students have been seniors for 25 years. I'm personally shocked that this school hasn't gone under investigation - there are actual teachers at this school, right?"

Watson's observations are completely right - as it turns out, students at East High tend to spend most if not all of their designated studying time practicing elaborate songs and choreographies to be performed around school, with no particular audience in mind. But it's not just the students who are at fault - several students caught wandering outside of their classes were told that their teachers encouraged such behaviour. Most students have been at East High for at least 10 years, consistently failing every exam given to them.

The principal of East High was confronted with Watson's remarks, to which he said "Don't listen to that old hag she's clearly delusional. Sounds like someone's just jealous they didn't get to go to an awesome school where you can sing wherever and whenever you want!"
East High Goes Private, Community Riots
   by Candice Owens - April 2, 2007
East High recently made the decision to change from a public school to a private school, and students will be charged tuition starting in the fall. Several parents have organized protests held outside the school, claiming that this change is completely unjustified.

But why is East High going private in the first place? According to the school principal, "We just wanted a really nice website to share our wildcat pride with the world, but the company we contacted to create our website, EARS, claimed they were only able to do so if our school was some sort of buisness that made money. I know it's a big change, but just look at the website we got out of it! It's phenomenal!"

When polled about the new school website, 100% of responses claimed it was the best website they had ever seen. "I love the slideshow on the homepage," one student claimed, "you can tell EARS put a lot of effort into the JQuery and Javascript."

"It's so responsive!" another student remarked. "I love how I can go on the website from my phone, tablet, or computer, and it always looks and functions great. If this website were graded, I'd give it an A+!"
